SLOS results from an autosomal recessive mutation on the 7-dehydrocholesterol reductase (DHCR7) gene.1-3

Deficient activity of DHCR7 impairs the final step in cholesterol biosynthesis4. This results in:

Low choletesterol levels1,5

A buildup of toxic cholesterol precursors 7-DHC (7-dehydrocholesterol) and 8-DHC (8-dehydrocholesterol)4,5

Bile acids are also impacted because cholesterol is needed for bile acid synthesis1,6

Cholesterol and
Bile Acid Biosynthesis

Low cholesterol levels and high concentrations of 7 DHC and 8-DHC are seen in patients with SLOS.1,5,8,9

Low cholesterol levels result in low levels of bile acids.2
